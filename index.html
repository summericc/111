Featured Project Showcase

Carbon Nanotube Applications in Next-Generation Battery Technology

An independent laboratory experiment investigating the conductivity, architecture, and energy-storage capabilities of carbon nanotubes to develop safer, faster-charging, and more sustainable alternatives to current battery models.

Electrochemistry
Hypothesis

Integrating multi-walled carbon nanotubes into the anode assembly will substantially reduce internal resistance and elevate electron transmission speeds, leading to higher cycle stability compared to traditional graphitic layouts.

Methodology

Synthesized slurry compositions using functionalized carbon nanotubes. Assembled laboratory-scale test cells to carefully run cyclic voltammetry and electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) benchmarks across variable load cycles.

Key Outcome

Observed a noticeable increase in current density retention over 100 continuous test charges. Data collected indicated a promising step forward in mitigating structural degradation pathways typical of lithium-ion baselines.
