Project Focus 01 // Materials Science

Carbon Nanotube Applications in Next-Generation Battery Technology

Electrochemistry

Independent experiment investigating structural frameworks, conductivity dynamics, and practical energy-storage metrics of carbon nanotubes. The objective centers on engineering sustainable, rapid-charging micro-battery configurations capable of replacing unstable classical graphite structures.

Hypothesis

Structural incorporation of functionalized carbon nanotubes minimizes internal core thermal degradation while optimizing electron flux vectors across consecutive load cycles.

Methodology

Formulated multi-walled nanotube slurry amalgams, coating test anodes for evaluation under regulated charge intervals via comparative impedance spectroscopy metrics.

Key Outcome

Data logs recorded verified optimizations in overall current retention parameters, presenting a viable model baseline for scaling advanced green battery options.
